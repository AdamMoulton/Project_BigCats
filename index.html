Big Cats

Tigers

Tigers are the largest of the cat species weighing up to 857 pounds and reaching lengths as high as 11 feet. They are apex predators who usually prey on animals such as deers and bovids. They are very territorial and solitary animals but are known to be very social as well requiring large areas of habitat. Tigers at one point in time once could be found all over Asia, roaming in countries like Turkey all the way to eastern Russia. Tigers,however, have lost almost 93% of their historic range and are now considered an endangered specie. There is often conflict between humans as they tend to go about in some of the most densely populated areas.

Jaguars

The Jaguar, falling under the Panthera genus along with the Tiger, is the third largest of the big cat species weighing up to 211 pounds and measuring lengths up to 6 feet from the nose to the base of the tail. They can be found hunting from the southwestern part of the United States, Mexico, and the central Americas. It is the only cat under the Panthera genus that is native to the Americas. Their conservation status is currently at near threatened status and population numbers continue to decline due to encounters with ranchers and farmers and habitat fragmentation.

Cheetahs

The cheetah is the last living member of the genus Acinonyx and can be found in sub-Saharan Africa. Although not as big as Jaguars and Tigers only weighing up to around 159 pounds and having lengths up to 7 feet, they are mostly known for their lightning speed. They are considered the fastest land animal averaging hunting speeds up to 40 mph and able to have short bursts of speed that can reach as high as 70 mph. Their slender bodies and light builds allow them to specialize in speed. Preying on antelopes and gazelles, they are also very similar to that of Tigers in that they are territorial while also sociable hunting in groups called “coalitions”. Their current conservation status is at vulnerable. Rampant hunting during the 20th century have caused a significant decline in population.

Notable Info

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • 3,890 tigers now exist in the wild
  • Top running speeds at 40 mph
  • Most Tigers live in Asia, specifically throughout Southeast Asia, China, Korea and Russia
  • Conservation status: Endangered

Notable Info

  • Scientific Name: Panthera onca
  • 15,000 jaguars left in the wild
  • Top running speeds at 30 mph
  • Significant numbers of jaguars are found only in remote regions of South and Central America—particularly in the Amazon Basin.
  • Conservation status: Near Threatened

Notable Info

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus
  • 6,600 cheetahs in 29 populations left in the wild
  • Top running speeds at 70 mph
  • Cheetahs are mostly found in sub-Saharan Africa and in eastern and southern African parks
  • Conservation status: Vulnerable
